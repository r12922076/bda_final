# Public-Evidence-Derived Personas

These personas are not interview respondents. They are synthetic personas derived from public evidence, product positioning, official documentation, and adjacent-product pricing. They are included to explain likely customer workflows, not to claim direct survey evidence.

| Persona ID | User type | Workflow data already produced | Main pain | Current workaround | PineGuard entry product | WTP interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Alert-heavy paid TradingView user | Alert events, webhook payloads, chart screenshots, strategy notes | Cannot easily know how many alerts later worked, failed, or became stale. | Manual screenshots, spreadsheets, memory, or ad hoc paper-trading notes. | Forward-Test Monitor | Indirect WTP from alert automation, journaling, and paid TradingView workflows. |
| P2 | Pine Script builder | Strategy Tester outputs, Pine code, parameter versions, backtest assumptions | Needs checks for repainting, lookahead, commission/slippage, and missing rules. | Manual code review, trial-and-error, separate Python notebooks. | Strategy Health Check | Indirect WTP from strategy-building and backtesting tools. |
| P3 | Paid-script buyer | Screenshots, received alerts, observed signals, claims from script creators | Cannot inspect invite-only source code and worries about black-box claims. | Paper trade for several weeks or ask for community opinions. | One-time Strategy Health Check | Plausible one-time report WTP, but direct buyer interviews are still needed. |
| P4 | Indicator creator | Strategy descriptions, alert templates, live-history samples | Wants trust without promising future returns. | Marketing claims, screenshots, or manual performance posts. | Creator Trust Report, later expansion | Expansion segment only; evidence is weaker than the alert-heavy user wedge. |

## How to use these personas in the report

Use them to explain customer workflows and product sequencing. Do not describe them as interview data. The initial beachhead remains P1: alert-heavy paid TradingView users.
